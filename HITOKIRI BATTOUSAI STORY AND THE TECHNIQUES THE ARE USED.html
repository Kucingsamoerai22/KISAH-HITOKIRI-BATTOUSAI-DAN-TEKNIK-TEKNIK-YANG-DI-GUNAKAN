ALL SAMURAI IN JAPAN FROM ERA HEIAN TO ERA MEIJI



MIYAMOTO MUSHASHI

The history of the samurai stretches back 700 years in the Japanese Empire. During that time, there were many great samurai who carved Japanese history. One of the greatest and most famous is Miyamoto Musashi. Musashi was a respected swordsman and ronin in Japan. His interesting life story made Musashi one of the most famous cultural icons in the Land of the Rising Sun. Miyamoto Musashi (宮本 武蔵), or simply Musashi, was a very famous samurai and ronin in medieval Japan. He is thought to have been born around 1584, and died in 1645. His real name was Shinmen Takezo. The word Musashi is another pronunciation of "Takezo" (kanji letters can have many pronunciations and meanings). Musashi has the full name Shinmen Musashi No Kami Fujiwara No Genshin.

SHIMAZU YOSHIHIRO

Shimazu Yoshihiro (島津 ​​義弘, 21 August 1535 – 30 August 1619) was a daimyo during the Japanese Civil War/Sengoku Period. The second son of Shimazu Takahisa and younger brother of Shimazu Yoshihisa, head of the Shimazu clan. Since he was young, he has shown his prowess as a soldier and followed his older brother in various battles. One of the most famous warlords of the Sengoku period, Shimazu Yoshihisa came from Satsuma Province. He was briefly married to his aunt. He launched a campaign to unify Kyushu, and experienced many victories. His clan controlled most of Kyushu for many years, but was eventually defeated by Toyotomi Hideyoshi. After the defeat, Yoshihisa is believed to have retired and become a Buddhist monk. He died peacefully.

DATE MASAMUNE

Date Masamune was the eldest son of Date Terumune, born at Yonezawa Castle (in modern Yamagata Prefecture). At the age of 14 in 1581 Masamune led his first campaign, helping his father against the Soma family. In 1584, at the age of 18, Masamune succeeded his father, Terumune, who chose to retire from his position as daimyo[2]. The Date family was founded in the early Kamakura period by Isa Tomomune, who originally came from the Isa district of Hitachi Province (now Ibaraki Prefecture). The family took its name from the Date district (now Fukushima Prefecture) of Mutsu Province, which had been given to Isa Tomomune by Minamoto no Yoritomo, the first syogun of Kamakura, for aid in the Genpei War (1180-1185) and in Minamoto no Yoritomo's struggle for power with his brother , Minamoto no Yoshitsune. Date Masamune's troops are identified by their black armor and gold headgear.
